Syd Barrett

“I’m disappearing, avoiding most things.”
1965, In Stanhope Gardens Lodon. Pink Floyd Music

A time line about of Syd Barrett's life:

  • 1946 - born in Cambridge / England
  • 1951 - family moved to 183 Hills Road
  • 1956 - got an ukulele
  • 1957 - got a banjo
  • 1960 - got a Hofner (acoustic guitar)
  • 1961 - father died of cancer
  • 1965 - joined a band "Pink Floyd", but it was called "The Tea Set"
  • 1967 - started dating Jenny Spires
  • 1968 - excluded from Pink Floyd
  • 1969 - solo carrier / first single Octopus
  • 1970 - released two albums "The Madcap Laughs" and "Barret"
  • 1978 - moved to Cambrigde to live with his mother (reclusive lifestyle and started painting)
  • 1982 - moved back to London
  • 1988 - released Opel album
  • 1996 - "Rock and Roll Hall of Fame" as a member of Pink Floyd
  • 2005 - biography "Madcap" by Tim Willis
  • 2006 - Died (pancreatic cancer)

"David is a great admirer of the late Syd Barrett, as both a person of great inner beauty whom he feels privileged to have personally known, and as an extremely talented songwriter who could have been one of the world's greatest musical talents had circumstances been different. David always comments on the way the lyrics just seemed to pour from Syd, and he certainly inspired David."

Phil Taylor (David Gilmour's equipment technician and expert)
